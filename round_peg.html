You are a round peg
(probably)

Every body is different

There is no single "correct" way to play the cello that fits every person. Our hands, arms, proportions and flexibility are all unique. Good technique begins by recognising that reality, not by ignoring it.

Principles, not positions

Good teachers do not teach fixed positions. They explain principles, demonstrate possibilities and guide experience. Technical instructions are signposts, not destinations.

Experience creates understanding

Real understanding comes from experience. As you learn, you gradually stop copying someone else's movements and begin discovering solutions that work naturally for your own body.

Your own best playing

The goal is never to become another cellist. It is to understand yourself well enough to become the best version of your own playing.

Remember the round peg

We spend far too much time trying to squeeze ourselves into square holes. Whenever technique becomes frustrating, remember this: we are all round pegs.
